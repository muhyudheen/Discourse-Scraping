What the Data Tells Us

Pull back from the individual and look at the shape of the whole. What does this data reveal about the culture of peer learning at IITM?

Second, it reveals a healthy reciprocity loop. The remarkably strong correlation between likes given and likes received (r = 0.87) suggests an ecosystem where appreciation flows back to those who give it. This positive feedback loop is what keeps contributors motivated.

Third, it reveals the hidden value of silent users. Those 37 silent readers and 64 lurkers are not freeloaders â€” they represent the students who needed the most help but lacked the confidence or time to ask. The fact that 8,128 hours of reading happened proves the forum served them too, even if invisibly.

Finally, it reveals that tenure beats intensity. The highest-engaged users are not those who had a single burst of activity â€” they are the ones who showed up day after day. Consistency, not heroics, is what builds a learning community.
